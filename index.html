please don't use obscure acronyms. just use the words.

Acronyms save a few seconds for the sender. They can cost minutes for everyone else.

Not everyone works in your department. Not everyone works in your industry. Not everyone interprets the same acronym the same way.

When communication matters, write the words.

Imagine a meeting where half the room is nodding along and nobody knows what KPI means. 🤦

❌ Don't do this

Alex 2:15 PM

Can you send me the PRD by EOD?

Sam 2:16 PM

What's PRD?

Alex 2:16 PM

Product Requirements Document.

Sam 2:17 PM

What's EOD?

Alex 2:17 PM

End of day.

Sam 2:18 PM

Right. I'll send it over.

Several messages later, the actual conversation begins. Sam could have started working minutes sooner — and Alex wouldn't have had to play acronym dictionary.

✅ Instead try this

Alex 2:15 PM

Can you send me the product requirements document by the end of the day?

Sam 2:15 PM

I'll have it to you by 4 PM.

Done.

The problem isn't that acronyms exist. The problem is assuming everyone knows which one you mean.

A simple rule

  • If the reader might not know the acronym — write it out.
  • If you aren't sure — write it out.
  • If it's the first time you're mentioning it — write it out.
  • If typing the full phrase takes less time than explaining it later — definitely write it out.

What about the obvious ones?

Everyone knows what USA, FBI, and NASA mean. Use those. The test is simple: would a new hire, a contractor, or someone from another team know it without looking it up?

If you have to ask "should I define this?" — define it. Your future self in a cross-functional meeting will thank you.

The internet has enough confusion already.

Use words.
